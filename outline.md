## 1. Strategic positioning (the through-line)

**The position the site has to communicate in one sentence:** *Industry-specific video craft and art, delivered at scale.*

This is the differentiated middle ground — premium craft + at-scale operational depth + sector-specific knowledge — that almost no competitor occupies. Pure craft boutiques (Buck, Ordinary Folk) signal "bespoke, expensive" but have no sector pages, no subscription, no operator architecture. Volume operators (Motion The Agency, Superside) have the structure but signal "affordable, commodity." Seven Bison has to read as both: serious craft from the first frame, with the architecture and proof points of an operator that handles ongoing relationships at scale.

**Core positioning copy:** Premium video, motion design and animation for B2B brands with complex, regulated, or hard-to-photograph subject matter. End-to-end creative partner — from research, strategy and concept through to final delivery. AI-native production, sector-specific craft.

**The three industries (the spine of the site):**
1. **Tech** — cybersecurity, fintech, computer software, SaaS
2. **Heavy industry**
3. **Healthcare & pharma**

These aren't aspirational — they're where the strongest portfolio sits. Each gets a dedicated subpage so we can demonstrate deep knowledge of the buyer's world.

**The AI angle — balanced, named, never headlined:**
- AI is the production engine. Two client benefits to lead with:
  1. Premium-looking work **without expensive video shoots** (the direct-client wedge — brand managers know what shoots cost)
  2. Faster turnaround at agency-quality
- AI is owned and named where relevant — not hidden, not buzzword-fronted

**Service hierarchy (order matters in nav and copy):**

Lead capability — frames the studio as a full creative partner, not a vendor:
1. **Creative storytelling** — from research, strategy and concept through to final delivery

Core craft disciplines (equal billing — shown side-by-side at top of services strip):

2. **2D/3D motion design**
3. **Cinematic AI image & video generation**

Supporting craft:

4. Short-form editing
5. 2D/3D character animation
6. VFX / compositing
7. Graphic design / 2D illustrations
8. 3D modelling

**Tone:** Confident, sober, specific. No agency hype. No AI buzzwords. Founder-led credibility carries weight (Szymon ex-Bloomberg UK + Getty Images editorial).

**Pricing:** No numbers anywhere on the public site. Studio Access is named and pitched as a first-class offering, but every commercial conversation routes to a call.

**Where the "at scale" signal comes from** (this matters — Seven Bison can't use the standard operator-tier signals):

| Standard operator signals — REJECT | Seven Bison's signals — USE |
|---|---|
| Headcount claims ("team of 50+") | Named blue-chip brand logos at volume |
| Volume claims ("500+ videos delivered") | Sector subpage depth — vocabulary that proves insider knowledge |
| Pricing visible ("from £X/month") | Studio Access named as a first-class offering with its own page |
| Money-back guarantees / free samples | Founder credibility (Bloomberg + Getty news editorial background) |
| Glossy "team" pages with stock-style photos | Customer stories with quantified outcomes from existing relationships |

## 5. Sector subpages (the conversion engine)

The three sector subpages are where Liza's LinkedIn prospects land and decide whether to book a call. They have to feel **specifically written for the buyer's world** — pain points named in their language, recurring content needs identified, anchor brands they recognise, work that maps to their use cases.

> ⚠️ **The copy for these pages is not templatable.** Each subpage needs a dedicated research pass to identify pain points, vocabulary, recurring content formats, and regulatory or technical context. A generic capabilities page on /healthcare-pharma will lose to a page that names HCP education, MOA animations, congress content, omnichannel HCP campaigns, and patient education in the language a Head of Digital at Roche actually uses. Same applies for cybersecurity, fintech, SaaS, and heavy industry.

> **Next deliverable from Claude:** sector-specific pain-point research, then subpage copy.

## 6. Studio Access page

The productised ongoing offer. Treated as a **first-class offering with its own page and IA presence**, not a sub-product of the studio. Half the "at scale" signal lives here.

- **Hero:** *"Studio Access — ongoing video, motion design and animation on a monthly cadence."*
- **What's included:**
  - Editing of existing footage (webinars, demos, founder clips, customer interviews)
  - Motion design and animation
  - Animated explainers
  - Product content
  - Cinematic AI image and video generation
  - Scoped to volume
- **How it works:** monthly cadence, dedicated team, predictable output. Three steps: brief → produce → deliver
- **Who it's for:** brands with recurring content needs — internal marketing/comms teams running multiple channels who don't want one-off vendor friction
- **Testimonials block:** 1–2 quotes from clients on long-term/ongoing work
- **Customer story:** at least one with quantified output — e.g. *"X assets delivered across Y months for [Brand]"* — to underline operator credibility
- **No pricing on the page.** CTA: "Talk to us about Studio Access"

## 9. Case study template

Each case study follows the same structure. **Treat case studies as editorial pieces, not template cards** — the craft signal lives partly here. Long-form scrolling pages with real writing, not bullet lists.

1. **Hero** — video embed or strong still
2. **One-line summary** — *"Explainer film for [X], a [sector] [company type]."*
3. **Brief / role / timeline** — short metadata block
4. **The piece itself** (or multiple pieces)
5. **Brief production note** — if AI was used in a way worth naming, name it

## 10. Testimonials and reviews

### Sources to use
- **Google reviews** — keep as a trust signal (rating + count near hero)
- **Clutch reviews** — keep as a trust signal (rating + count near hero)
- **Written testimonials** — from letters, emails, LinkedIn DMs where Szymon has permission to publish. **These are the heavy artillery for direct client conversion**

### Why written testimonials matter more than star ratings
Star ratings work for low-consideration purchases. For a B2B buyer about to commit thousands of dollars and weeks of project time, a named quote from a peer — a marketing director at Roche, a brand manager at Hilti, a CMO at a fintech — carries more weight than any number of anonymous stars.

### Placement strategy
- **Homepage:** 3–4 strongest testimonials, sector-balanced (Section 9)
- **Each sector subpage:** 1–2 sector-relevant testimonials — this is where they earn their keep
- **Studio Access page:** 1–2 testimonials about ongoing/long-term partnerships
- **Case studies:** one quote per case study where available
- **Trust strip near hero:** Google + Clutch ratings only — small, supporting role

A short list of which letter/email testimonials are publishable, with: sender name, title, company, sector, quote text. Pull from existing inbox archive when there's time.

## 12. Notes for Filip

### Design language — "Industry-specific video craft and art at scale"

Seven Bison's visual position sits in a gap no single competitor fills cleanly. **Filip will need to compose from multiple references rather than clone one.** The combination is the differentiation.

#### Reference set — finish and craft (visual ceiling)
The look-and-feel target. Dark, cinematic, editorial typography, work-first, minimal copy, no decoration. Treat these as the bar for visual finish:

- **Buck** — [buck.co](https://buck.co) — for navigation simplicity, work-first hero, confidence
- **Tendril** — [tendril.ca](https://tendril.ca) — for cinematic dark sophistication, 3D treatment
- **ManvsMachine** — [manvsmachine.co.uk](https://manvsmachine.co.uk) — for typography and editorial confidence

#### Reference set — craft at scale (operator credibility without losing finish)
Studios that signal "serious clients + premium craft + reliable production at scale" — the closest analogues to Seven Bison's positioning:

- **Imaginary Forces** — [imaginaryforces.com](https://imaginaryforces.com) — title sequences for Stranger Things, Mad Men. Multi-office, serious clients at volume, never looks cheap
- **Antibody** — [antibody.tv](https://antibody.tv) — VFX + motion, premium entertainment finish at production scale

#### Reference set — structural bones only (NOT visual)
For sector page architecture, Studio Access framing, customer story patterns, trust badge placement:

- **Motion The Agency** — [motiontheagency.com](https://motiontheagency.com) — steal the IA (sector landing pages, named subscription offer, customer stories with quantified outcomes, Clutch badge strip). **Reject everything visual** — purple gradients, SaaS-coded icons, glassmorphism, "from £X" pricing

#### What to explicitly avoid
- **Golden Wolf** — too irreverent/youthful for pharma + cybersecurity + heavy industry buyers
- **Ordinary Folk** — too boutique-feeling, doesn't signal scale
- **Motion The Agency visual treatment** — SaaS-coded, lowers perceived tier
- **Generic AI-studio visual tropes** — generative gradients, glassmorphism, "futuristic" sci-fi type, particle backgrounds, glowing accents
- **Volume-tier signals** — character-driven 2D as the hero look, money-back guarantees, "from $X" pricing, free sample lead magnets, video cost calculators, headcount claims
- **Stock-feeling "team" or "office" photography**

#### Visual rules
- **Dark mode default** (or a sophisticated light treatment with serious typography — not bright white)
- **Oversized video on hero**, autoplay, no controls visible
- **Typography:** editorial serif for headlines OR confident sans-serif at large weight with editorial spacing. Not "SaaS sans-serif"
- **Sparse use of colour** — let the work bring the colour
- **No decorative gradients, glow effects, or particle backgrounds**
- **Generous negative space** (whether dark or light)
- **Case studies as long-form scrolling pages with editorial writing**, not template cards

### Motion-first
Homepage and sector pages lean heavily on autoplaying video. The work has to sell the work. Don't bury reels behind play buttons. **The craft signal in the first three seconds of the hero reel does more work than any headline.**
